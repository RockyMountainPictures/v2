About Me

A little autobiography

This was an assignment that I did in grade 9, so I've tweaked it since and will keep updating it every year.

Hello World.

I've always been interested in programming, but never delved into it much. It seems too complicated from an outsider's point of view. Anyway, you can call me Max. I attend a school in the SD43 cachement. I've always been interested in technology and how it works. I assembled my first computer at age 12, deconstructed a TV for a project in grade 7, and started constructing this website for another project in grade 8.

While I like the intracasies of tech, I also enjoy gaming on said tech. Minecraft is still one of my favourite games to spend my free time. And since it's a sandbox game, there are endless possibilities on how to spend it! Over the 5+ years I've played it, I've spent over 1000 hours destroying, building, killing, and creating.

Sometimes, you just have to relax and spend time away from technology. I always love a nice walk in the forest and enjoying nature. Whenever my family and I go on a big trip, I like bring my camera along and take pictures of the scenery. Photography is a hobby I will always hold onto, and it keeps stories in the photos you take. My dream job would be a nature photographer for the National Geographic or a similar company.

I also do other activities such as mountain biking, skiing, running, badminton and squash.

I'm not a thing for quotes, but this one from Marques Brownlee (MKBHD) is one that has stuck with me the most. It seems easy to be in control your life and schedule, but it's easier said than done. At least once a day, something that I wanted to do always gets pushed back because of someone else's plans, whether it's personal or school related. But it's okay to have something slip through and mess up your day. It gives life an interesting twist and helps you prepare for other deviations that will occur in the future.

I watch YouTube a bit too much, but there is a lot of content that I love. One of my favourite videos that I've come across is one from a fellow photographer/videographer. Johnny Harris works for Vox, but also has is own Youtube channel where he does some interesting takes on photo/video related topics. One of his best videos is his opinion on famous photos. It's honestly worth a watch if you are someone that wants to take that famous picture themselves.

About my favourite hobby

Photography is a methodical art in my opinions. I get to appreciate nature and enjoy the outdoors as I take snippets of thier life. I then get home and relax with a cup of hot chocolate as I edit them in my cozy den. I would love to do that everyday as my job (but let's be realistic).

I entered this hobby in grade 8 as a passion project in school. I learnt all about how to use a camera, framing my photos, editing them with my parent's Canon acient XTi. I even made a guide to build a pinhole camera. I had learnt a lot of valuable information, and consolodated it into a website called Rocky Mountain Pictures.

Over the next few years I became more interested in photography, learning new techniques in Photo 9, and keeping the camera within reach wherever I went, incase I saw an interesting photo opprotunity.

In 2018, I began saving my pennies for my very own camera, and in May of 2019, I bought my very own camera; a used Fujifilm X-T20. I was amazed on how much more I could do with a more modern camera built for portability and flexibility. Right this very moment, my camera is out on the porch creating a story of the City of Vernon.
